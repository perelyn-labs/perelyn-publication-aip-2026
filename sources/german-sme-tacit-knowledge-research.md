# German SME (Mittelstand) Tacit Knowledge Preservation: Comprehensive Research
**Research Date:** May 19, 2026

## 1. THE SCALE OF THE CRISIS: DEMOGRAPHIC STATISTICS

### Babyboomer Retirement Wave
- **13.4 million** workers (Erwerbspersonen) will exceed the legal retirement age of 67 by 2039 (Destatis, based on 2024 microcensus). This is **31% of all workers** available to the labor market.
- **16.5 million** Babyboomers (born 1955-1969) will retire by 2036 -- but only **12.5 million** younger workers enter the workforce (IW Koeln).
- **4.7 million** Germans will exit employment through retirement between 2024-2028 (Federal Employment Agency).
- **80,000 people** leave the workforce monthly due to retirement (DGUV).
- By 2035, **1 in 4** Germans will be aged 67+ (vs. 1 in 5 in 2024) (Destatis).
- Old-age dependency ratio: **35.9%** by late 2025 -- record high (Eurostat).

### Manufacturing and Trades Specific
- **200,000+** skilled craft positions unfilled (ZDH estimate; 125,500 officially registered Dec 2024).
- **125,000 craft businesses** (>10% of all) face generational succession in next 5 years.
- **80,000 craft jobs lost** in 2024 alone because owners found no successors ("stilles Sterben").
- **728,000-768,000** skilled positions projected missing by 2027-2028 (IW/BDA forecasts).
- **600,000 SMEs** need succession solutions by 2025 (KfW).
- In manufacturing, **15% of workers** will disappear from the German labor market by 2030 (IAB).
- Only **17% of German manufacturing companies** actively deploy AI; 40% in discussion phase; 40% struggle to find AI-qualified workforce.

### Knowledge Loss Dimension
- IHK Berlin: By 2036, ~20 million Babyboomers retire, taking decades of undocumented experiential knowledge.
- 57% of non-fillable positions are in **dual-vocational-training professions** -- exactly where tacit knowledge is most critical.
- SMEs generate >1/3 of total German company revenue and employ ~60% of all social-insurance-contributing workers.

## 2. GERMAN AI STARTUPS FOR KNOWLEDGE PRESERVATION

### A. BLOCKBRAIN (Stuttgart)
**Website:** blockbrain.ai
**Founded:** 2022
**Founders:** Antonius Gress (ex-Bosch), Mattias Protzmann (co-founder of Statista), Nam Hai Ngo
**Funding:** EUR 17.5M Series A (Feb 2026), led by Alstin Capital (Munich) + 13books Capital (London)
**Revenue growth:** 5x in 2025

**What it does:**
- Creates "digital knowledge twins" of domain experts within weeks
- Stores expert information, maps thinking patterns and decision-making logic
- Knowledge Bots make stored experiential knowledge available across teams/locations
- No-code platform -- Knowledge Bots created in <5 minutes without coding
- Core IP: knowledge extraction, indexing and retrieval BEFORE the LLM -- reduces hallucinations

**Customers:** Roland Berger, Bosch, Bosch Rexroth, Kaercher, fischer, HARTING, Eberspaecher, Bardehle Pagenberg, CHG Meridian, Domcura, Seifert Logistics
**Reported results:** Seifert Logistics CEO reports **15% weekly time savings** through digital twin use
**Target:** Companies with 50-5,000 employees, EUR 5M-5B revenue; also serving smaller firms (law practices, startups)
**Pricing:** Enterprise-level; "initial tickets typically EUR 2M-8M" (from one source -- likely misquoted or referring to total contract value). No public SME pricing found.
**Language:** German + English
**SME accessibility:** Designed for Mittelstand but pricing unclear; no-code aspect lowers technical barrier

### B. INTERLOOM (Munich)
**Website:** interloom.ai
**Founded:** 2024
**Founder/CEO:** Fabian Jakobi
**Funding:** EUR 14.2M ($16.5M) Seed round (March 2026), led by DN Capital + Bek Ventures + Air Street Capital

**What it does:**
- Enterprise operations platform that captures expert knowledge and turns it into "permanent memory" for AI agents
- Creates a "Context Graph" -- a continuously evolving model of operational decisions
- Ingests millions of real cases: support emails, service tickets, call transcripts, work orders
- Extracts patterns of how expert workers actually resolve problems
- Analogous to how Google Maps improves routes based on real-time traffic
- Key insight: "70% of operational decisions are never formally documented" (Jakobi)

**Customers:** Zurich Insurance, JLL, Fiege (logistics), Commerzbank, Volkswagen
**Validation:** Won Zurich Insurance internal AI competition against **2,000 competing AI startups** for underwriting use case
**Pricing:** Not publicly available; enterprise-focused
**Language:** English primarily
**SME accessibility:** Currently enterprise-focused (insurance, logistics, automotive). Not yet targeting SME manufacturing.
**Knowledge types:** Operational decision patterns, case resolution workflows, process knowledge

### C. KNOWRON (Munich)
**Website:** knowron.com
**Founded:** 2020
**Founders:** Fabian Pelzl, Arturo Buitrago Mendez, Ali Kareem Raja (met at CDTM Munich)
**Funding:** EUR 1.8M seed round

**What it does:**
- AI-powered personal assistant for service technicians (fits in every pocket)
- Turns lengthy product documentation into searchable step-by-step instructions
- NLP-powered search, voice-based data capture, factory layout visualization
- Troubleshooting question trees to narrow down problems
- Recommends and sources spare parts
- Analytics to uncover knowledge gaps and improve training

**Customers:** Voith, E.ON, ASMPT (semiconductor equipment)
**Industries:** Semiconductors, machinery/equipment, power/utilities, food & beverage
**Pricing:** Not public; demo-based sales model ("no commitment, no obligation -- just a demo")
**Language:** German + English (Munich-based, German customers)
**SME accessibility:** Mobile-first, designed for frontline workers -- potentially accessible to smaller manufacturers

### D. WORKERBASE (Munich)
**Website:** workerbase.com
**Founded:** 2017
**Founders:** Norman Hartmann, Thorsten Krueger, Hamid Reza Monadjem
**Funding:** EUR 10M Series A (from Almaz Capital, Porsche Ventures, Bayern Kapital, Point Nine)

**What it does:**
- Connected worker platform: Dynamic Process Execution (DPE)
- Connects people, processes, machines, and materials in real-time
- AI algorithms analyze employee skills, location, availability to assemble task forces
- AI-Powered Work Instructions: auto-creates step-by-step instructions from PDFs (via Vertex AI)
- Multi-modal Chat Assistant for assembly questions
- "SpeedUp" button: instant AI recommendations on machine faults
- Knowledge capturing: users annotate manuals with text/audio/pictures/video -- builds tribal knowledge library

**Customers:** Porsche, Siemens, PON.bike, GKN Powder Metallurgy, Gazelle
**Results:** Siemens and Porsche report "significant improvements in workflow efficiency and worker satisfaction"
**Pricing:** Not public
**Language:** German + English
**SME accessibility:** Large-enterprise focused (Porsche, Siemens); unclear if affordable for smaller SMEs

## 3. NON-STARTUP TOOLS AND SOLUTIONS

### E. synartIQ + WitronIQ (Bielefeld)
**Website:** synartiq.de
**20+ years experience** in knowledge management

**What it does:**
- WitronIQ: software for self-directed knowledge transfer process
- Guides knowledge providers through structured transfer: collection -> personal knowledge map -> transfer plan
- Uses guiding questions to surface hidden experiential knowledge
- Creates personal knowledge maps including tacit knowledge
- Pool of transfer methods with templates, tutorials, briefs

**Pricing:** Starting at **EUR 299/year per user license** (standard version)
**Language:** German
**SME accessibility:** VERY accessible -- affordable pricing, German-language, designed for knowledge transfer
**Knowledge types:** Tacit + explicit; focuses on person-to-person knowledge transfer process
**Limitation:** NOT AI-powered; structured methodology tool, not LLM-based

### F. iSAX "weasl" (Dresden/Saxony)
**Website:** isax.com
**20+ years** industrial IoT expertise

**What it does:**
- "weasl" = flexible worker assistance system for manufacturing
- Digital work instructions for shop floor
- Structured knowledge transfer from knowledge carriers to knowledge recipients
- Makes knowledge available independently of personnel changes
- Focus on discrete manufacturing in DACH region

**Pricing:** Not public
**Language:** German
**SME accessibility:** Manufacturing SME focused; worker assistance on shop floor
**Knowledge types:** Process/procedural knowledge, work instructions

### G. Eschbach "Shiftconnector Smart Search" (Bad Saeckingen)
**Website:** eschbach.com
**Case study:** Bayer CropScience

**What it does:**
- AI-infused Smart Search within Plant Process Management (PPM) solution
- Captures implicit knowledge from shift operations (24/7 plants)
- Transforms information repository into intelligent knowledge hub
- Enables faster troubleshooting by accessing experienced teams' documented resolutions

**Pricing:** Enterprise
**Language:** German/English
**SME accessibility:** More suited to larger process manufacturing plants
**Knowledge types:** Shift operation knowledge, troubleshooting, process knowledge

### H. Augmentir (US-based, serves German market)
**Website:** augmentir.com

**What it does:**
- AI-native connected worker platform
- Captures tribal knowledge from subject matter experts -> sharable corporate asset
- Digitizes and optimizes frontline worker processes "from hire to retire"
- Digital work instructions, remote assist, collaboration tools
- Generative AI turns hidden expertise into corporate asset

**Pricing:** Subscription model; claims "designed for industrial companies of any size" -- but no public pricing
**Language:** English (international)
**SME accessibility:** Claims any-size suitability
**Knowledge types:** Process knowledge, work instructions, troubleshooting

## 4. GOVERNMENT-FUNDED PROGRAMS AND INITIATIVES

### I. Mittelstand-Digital Zentren (BMWK)
- **~30 centers nationwide**, ~100 AI trainers
- **FREE** workshops, demonstrations, individual consultation
- Current network runs through **end of 2026** (then new competition)
- Since June 2024: increasing focus on AI and AI-readiness
- Includes **Mittelstand-Digital Zentrum Handwerk** -- specific for crafts
- Evaluation shows: programs measurably increase revenue for participating SMEs
- New funding competition launched Dec 2025 for regional centers (sketches due March 31, 2026)
- **Access:** Free, German language, open to all SMEs
- **Limitation:** Advisory only -- does not provide tools or software

### J. ProKI-Netz (BMBF)
- **8 demonstration/transfer centers** across Germany (coordinated from RWTH Aachen)
- Funded since October 1, 2022
- Focuses on AI in manufacturing: cutting, joining, forming, coating
- Offers: information events, industrial-scale demos, qualification programs, individual consultations
- Located at: TU Berlin, TU Dresden, TU Darmstadt, RWTH Aachen, Uni Hannover, others
- **Goal:** Help manufacturing SMEs utilize AI potentials, improve value creation
- **Access:** Free for SMEs
- **Limitation:** Focus on manufacturing processes (quality, productivity), not specifically knowledge preservation

### K. KI4KMU (BMBF)
- "Research, Development and Use of AI Methods in SMEs"
- **Funding:** Up to 50% of eligible project costs; startups up to EUR 100,000/year
- Two-stage application via DLR Projekttraeger
- Supports SMEs in accelerated technology transfer
- **Access:** Application-based; German SMEs can apply with academic/industry partners

### L. AI-Storytelling Project (Fraunhofer IFF / BMBF)
- **"Vom Erfahrungswissen zum digital nutzbaren Know-how"**
- Runs 2025-2028; part of "Generative KI fuer den Mittelstand" program
- Uses narrative methods (storytelling, triad conversations) to surface unconscious knowledge
- VR for depicting complex work processes
- Combines BaSyx + KIWAI software platforms with AR and digital twins
- **Target:** Manufacturing SMEs, automotive, mechanical engineering, energy technology
- **Access:** Research project -- not yet a commercial product

### M. STARK-Buddy Project (Fraunhofer IPK / BMFTR)
- **Kicked off March 18, 2026** at Production Technology Center Berlin
- Developing company-specific, speech-based AI assistant ("STARK-Buddy")
- Smart glasses guide specialists in assembly
- Functions as moderator and mentor in knowledge securing/transfer
- **Partners:** BSP Business School, TU Berlin, Harms & Wende GmbH, FBT Feinblechtechnik GmbH
- **Funding:** ~EUR 2M via ESF Plus + BMFTR "Future of Work" program
- **Duration:** Until December 31, 2028
- **Target:** Manufacturing SMEs
- **Access:** Research project -- not yet a product

### N. WIN-Netzwerk Fulda (BIBB / BMFSFJ)
- Regional network for knowledge transfer + skilled worker retention
- Workshops and mentoring programs for SMEs
- Funded ~EUR 560,000 over 3 years (July 2025 - June 2028)
- Focuses on: knowledge transfer from Babyboomers, international worker integration, succession
- **Access:** Free for SMEs in Fulda region
- **Limitation:** Regional (Fulda), not technology-focused

### O. Online-Digitalisierungswerkstatt 2025/26 (Mittelstand-Digital Zentrum Handwerk)
- 12-month free online workshop: "KI -- neuer Problemloeser im Handwerk"
- Started March 2025; participating businesses implement a concrete AI project
- Expert support from KI trainers and digitalization experts
- Specific session: **"Wenn der Kollege geht -- Wie KI Wissen im Betrieb bewahrt"**
- **Access:** Free, German, open to all craft businesses
- **Language:** German only

## 6. KEY ACADEMIC RESEARCH

### Research Papers Directly Relevant

1. **"Documenting SME Processes with Conversational AI: From Tacit Knowledge to BPMN"** (Radhakrishnan, Aarhus University, arXiv 2512.05122, Nov 2025)
   - LLM-driven conversational assistant captures tacit knowledge, converts to BPMN 2.0
   - Proof-of-concept: produced accurate AS-IS model + improved TO-BE variant in ~12 minutes
   - **SME-friendly budget** for API costs
   - Key finding: conversational LLMs lower skill and cost barriers to process documentation

2. **"Knowledge Management in Manufacturing: Current Practices, Barriers, and Automation Potential for LLM-Supported Systems"** (MDPI, 2025)
   - Survey of German manufacturing companies
   - Barriers: fragmented documentation, reliance on interpersonal transfer, uneven digital KM tools

3. **"Unlocking AI-based Knowledge Management Potential for SMEs: Exploring Semantic Search Adoption"** (WI 2024 conference)
   - Multiple case study of German manufacturing B2B SMEs
   - Only 5.8% of businesses have applied AI; most developed solutions are not in-house

4. **"Knowledge sharing in manufacturing using LLM-powered tools"** (2024, PMC)
   - User study + model benchmarking for manufacturing knowledge sharing

5. **"Reinterpret Corporate Tacit Knowledge Using LLMs"** (industry-science.com)
   - Framework for using LLMs to extract and reinterpret corporate tacit knowledge

## 8. SYNTHESIS: GAP ANALYSIS FOR GERMAN SMEs

### What exists:
| Solution | Type | Tacit Knowledge? | SME Affordable? | German? | Manufacturing? |
|----------|------|------------------|-----------------|---------|---------------|
| Blockbrain | AI platform | Yes (knowledge twins) | Unclear (enterprise-priced?) | Yes | Partial |
| Interloom | AI platform | Yes (Context Graph) | Enterprise only | English | No (ops focus) |
| KNOWRON | AI assistant | Partial (documentation search) | Possibly | Yes | Yes (service) |
| Workerbase | Connected worker | Partial (tribal knowledge capture) | Enterprise | Yes | Yes |
| synartIQ WitronIQ | Methodology tool | Yes (structured interviews) | Yes (EUR 299/yr) | Yes | Generic |
| iSAX weasl | Worker assistance | Partial (work instructions) | SME-targeted | Yes | Yes |
| Augmentir | Connected worker | Partial | Claims any size | English | Yes |
| Fraunhofer projects | Research | Yes | Free (research) | Yes | Yes |
| Mittelstand-Digital | Advisory | No (advisory only) | Free | Yes | Partial |
| ProKI-Netz | Transfer center | No (AI in production) | Free | Yes | Yes |

### Critical gaps for German SMEs:
1. **No affordable, German-language, AI-powered tool specifically for capturing tacit manufacturing knowledge in SMEs** -- this is the central finding
2. Blockbrain comes closest but appears enterprise-priced
3. synartIQ is affordable but not AI-powered
4. KNOWRON focuses on documentation retrieval, not tacit knowledge capture
5. Government programs are advisory or research-stage, not deployable tools
6. Fraunhofer projects (AI-Storytelling, STARK-Buddy) won't deliver products until 2028+
7. The 125,000 craft businesses facing succession have NO dedicated AI tool
8. Language barrier: many tools are English-first

### The paradox:
- German SMEs are the MOST affected by tacit knowledge loss (demographic crisis + knowledge-intensive production)
- But they are the LEAST served by existing solutions (enterprise pricing, English language, research-only projects)
- The 43% with no AI plans are exactly those most at risk of catastrophic knowledge loss
