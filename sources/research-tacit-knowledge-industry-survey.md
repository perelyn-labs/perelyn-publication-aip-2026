# Industry Survey: Commercial Products, Startups, and Solutions for Tacit Knowledge Capture in Manufacturing

**Date:** 2026-05-18
**Purpose:** Research survey for AI4SE GI KI 2026 workshop paper context

---

## 1. Executive Summary: The Scale of the Problem

### Economic Impact
- **$92 billion/year** lost in manufacturing due to knowledge management failures (Morphik AI analysis)
- **$31.5 billion/year** lost by Fortune 500 companies due to employees not sharing knowledge (Nuclino/Deloitte)
- **$10 trillion** projected global cost of tacit knowledge loss by 2025 (World Economic Forum, 2016 Global Risks Report)
- **$1.4 trillion** estimated broader tribal knowledge problem in manufacturing (Dovient analysis)

### Workforce Crisis ("Silver Tsunami")
- **3.8 million** manufacturing jobs need filling by 2033; 2.8 million are direct replacements for retirees (Manufacturing Institute + Deloitte)
- **25%** of U.S. manufacturing workforce is over 55; up from 14% in 2000
- **10,000 Baby Boomers** retiring daily in the U.S.
- **70%** of critical operational knowledge in manufacturing is undocumented (multiple sources)
- **57%** of institutional knowledge in brown-stack industries is at risk in the coming decade (McKinsey)
- Only **57%** of expertise from "founding generation" engineers has been transferred to younger hires (McKinsey)

### Key Framing
- McKinsey: simultaneous "skills tsunami" and "silver tsunami" require immediate strategic action
- California Management Review (March 2026): "Tacit Knowledge Is Your Next Competitive Moat" -- argues that in the age of agentic AI, unique organizational expertise is the defining advantage
- OMERS Ventures investment thesis: vertical AI startups that codify tribal knowledge create "completely new ways of working" and build deep competitive moats
- Configit (2026 prediction): AI will become deeply embedded in manufacturing operations; digitizing tribal knowledge will accelerate as a competitive necessity

## 2. Dedicated Tacit Knowledge Capture Startups

### 2.1 Interloom (Munich, Germany)
- **What:** AI platform that builds "context graphs" -- continuously updated maps of how problems actually get resolved within an organization
- **Technology:** Ingests millions of operational records (support emails, service tickets, call transcripts, work orders) to map tacit operational decision paths. CEO Fabian Jakobi likens it to "Google Maps for operations"
- **Funding:** $16.5M (March 2026) led by DN Capital + Bek Ventures + Air Street Capital. Prior $3M seed in March 2024
- **Customers:** Commerzbank, Volkswagen, Zurich Insurance, JLL, Fiege (logistics)
- **Key Result:** At Commerzbank, reduced gap between documented and actual operational knowledge from ~50% to 5%
- **Competitive win:** Beat 2,000+ AI startups in Zurich Insurance company-wide AI competition for underwriting use case
- **Stage:** Seed/Early growth, live with "several large European enterprises"
- **Focus:** Enterprise operations broadly (not manufacturing-specific, but applicable)

### 2.2 Blockbrain (Stuttgart, Germany)
- **What:** Enterprise Generative AI platform deploying "Knowledge Bots" that capture, secure, and leverage internal corporate expertise
- **Technology:** Autonomous AI agents trained on in-house, non-public procedures, logs, and multimodal data. Resembles an LLM but is grounded in proprietary organizational knowledge
- **Funding:** EUR 17.5M Series A (Feb 2026) led by Alstin Capital + 13books Capital
- **Customers:** Roland Berger, Bosch, Bosch Rexroth, Kaercher, Fischer, Harting, Eberspaecher, Bardehle Pagenberg, CHG Meridian, Domcura, Seifert Logistics
- **Recognition:** Winner of Siemens 2025 Industrial AI Award for knowledge capture on the shop floor
- **Stage:** Series A, actively deployed in German industrial companies
- **Differentiation:** Strong focus on manufacturing/industrial shop floor use cases

### 2.3 Murray Mentor (Carmel, Indiana, USA)
- **What:** AI-powered "digital mentor" delivering real-time, in-ear voice guidance to shop-floor workers
- **Technology:** Proprietary AI that digitizes expertise through voice-activated, real-time assistance; learns from experienced workers and provides contextual guidance to newer employees; creates a "digital apprenticeship" system
- **Funding:** Backed by Fireroad Ventures and Start Something Ventures (amount undisclosed, early stage)
- **Customers:** Early paying customers across food & beverage, automotive, chemical manufacturing
- **Key Result:** In pilot deployments, reduced training cycles and helped companies protect hundreds of thousands of dollars annually
- **Stage:** Early-stage startup with first revenue
- **Differentiation:** Voice-first, in-ear delivery designed for shop floor; most direct "digital apprenticeship" analogy

### 2.4 Tacit AI (USA)
- **What:** Industrial Decision Engine for equipment maintenance and operational excellence
- **Technology:** Multimodal intelligence for industrial optimization -- detects, investigates, resolves, and prevents equipment issues
- **Funding:** $12.3M total raised
- **Stage:** Growth-stage startup
- **Focus:** More equipment/maintenance-focused than pure knowledge capture, but addresses the knowledge component of maintenance expertise

### 2.5 Starmind / Capacity (Zurich, Switzerland -- acquired)
- **What:** AI-powered platform that creates a real-time map of employee expertise by analyzing actual work activities
- **Technology:** Maps expertise dynamically across enterprise tools; connects employees to the most qualified internal experts in real-time. Embeds directly into Microsoft Teams and SharePoint
- **Funding:** $15M raised (Series A -- record for European AI at the time)
- **Customers:** Swiss Re, PepsiCo, Mondelez International, KSB, Draeger, Swisscom
- **Manufacturing use:** Specific manufacturing solutions for connecting shop floor workers to distributed expertise
- **Status:** Acquired by Capacity (U.S.-based support automation platform), now integrated into broader platform
- **Differentiation:** "People-to-people" knowledge routing (vs. document-based capture); identifies who knows what in real-time

## 3. Connected Worker Platforms (with Knowledge Capture Features)

### Market Size
- **$8.6-10B** in 2025, projected to reach **$20-28B** by 2030 (multiple analyst firms)
- Growing at **23-25% CAGR**

### 3.1 Augmentir (USA)
- **What:** Self-described "world's only AI-native connected worker platform" -- from "hire to retire"
- **Technology:** Combines Generative and Agentic AI with skills management, digital workflow, collaboration, and knowledge sharing. "Augie" is their industrial Generative AI assistant. Industrial AI Agent Studio enables no-code AI agent creation
- **Key Feature for Knowledge:** AI bots capture tribal knowledge and deliver real-time decision support to workers on the job. Continuous skills tracking creates a knowledge asset from daily operations
- **Pricing:** Custom enterprise pricing, estimated $25K-$100K+/year for mid-size deployments
- **Recognition:** 2023 Connected Worker Company of the Year (Frost & Sullivan)
- **Stage:** Mature, established enterprise platform
- **Differentiation:** AI-native from the ground up (not bolted on); skills intelligence layer tracks what each worker knows and can do

### 3.2 Poka (Quebec, Canada)
- **What:** Enterprise AI-augmented connected worker platform -- "knowledge-sharing and collaborative problem solving" specialist
- **Technology:** Central hub for digital work instructions, troubleshooting solutions, and critical operational knowledge accessible from the factory floor
- **Customers:** 2,300+ customers worldwide including Nestle, Mars, Bosch Automotive, Danone, L'Oreal, Tetra Pak, Johnson & Johnson
- **Results:** 54% increase in mean time between repair; 8% gain in direct productivity
- **Recognition:** Best Enterprise Connected Worker Platform by G2 (Fall 2024)
- **Stage:** Enterprise scale, deployed in hundreds of global factories
- **Differentiation:** Strong collaborative knowledge sharing -- workers share solutions peer-to-peer on the platform

### 3.3 Dozuki (San Luis Obispo, California, USA)
- **What:** AI-powered connected worker platform digitizing knowledge and scaling workforce training
- **Technology:** Digital work instructions, document control, knowledge management, learning pathways, operational workflows, and worker collaboration. AI-powered SOP extraction from legacy PDFs
- **Founded:** 2011 (originated from iFixit)
- **Stage:** Late-stage, mature platform
- **Differentation:** "Best for digital work instructions in manufacturing"; strong emphasis on media-rich, standardized instructions

### 3.4 Tulip (Boston, Massachusetts, USA)
- **What:** AI-native composable frontline operations platform
- **Technology:** No-code platform allowing factories to build custom apps. Integrates with machines, sensors, and systems. AI Chat for natural language queries. Computer vision and ML models built-in. GxP-ready for pharma/regulated industries
- **Key Feature for Knowledge:** Converts PDF SOPs to interactive apps; best-practice app library; AI-powered SOP generation
- **Partnership:** Deep integration with NVIDIA AI
- **Stage:** Well-funded, enterprise-scale (notable investors include Andreessen Horowitz)
- **Differentiation:** Composable/no-code approach lets each factory customize; strong pharma/regulated industry focus

### 3.5 MaintainX (USA)
- **What:** AI-powered CMMS and connected worker platform for maintenance and operations
- **Funding:** $254M total raised; $2.5B valuation (as of Series D, July 2025)
- **Customers:** 11,000+ companies worldwide managing 11M+ assets
- **Results:** 34% reduction in unplanned downtime; 15% increase in production capacity; 32% savings in monthly maintenance costs
- **Stage:** Unicorn-status ($2.5B valuation), largest pure-play in the space
- **Differentiation:** Largest scale; strong maintenance knowledge capture through work order history and AI-powered insights

### 3.6 Redzone / QAD (USA -- acquired)
- **What:** Connected workforce platform for production, quality, and maintenance teams
- **Technology:** Combines frontline workflows, performance data, and AI insights
- **Status:** Acquired by QAD (February 2023)
- **Stage:** Enterprise, integrated into QAD's broader manufacturing suite

### 3.7 Plex Connected Worker / Rockwell Automation (USA)
- **What:** Connected worker solution within Rockwell's industrial automation ecosystem
- **Technology:** Guided and Interactive Work Instructions with AI-driven insights and predictive maintenance
- **Key Feature for Knowledge:** "Creates and captures the corpus of manufacturing knowledge in an organic way" through daily work execution
- **Stage:** Enterprise, backed by Rockwell Automation's massive industrial install base
- **Differentiation:** Deep integration with Rockwell's PLC, MES, and automation infrastructure

## 4. Voice-First and Multimodal Knowledge Capture

### 4.1 Datch (Canada/USA)
- **What:** Industrial "Voice-Visual" AI assistant for hands-free knowledge capture
- **Technology:** Voice interface captures real-time sequence-of-events data during equipment breakdowns and maintenance. Workers speak into the system; AI structures and indexes the information
- **Funding:** $15M total ($3.2M seed, $10M Series A from Blackhorn Ventures)
- **Customers:** ConEd, Singapore Airlines, ABB Robotics, New York Power Authority
- **Stage:** Series A, deployed in mining, energy, and manufacturing
- **Differentiation:** Purpose-built voice-visual interface for industrial noise environments

### 4.2 Taggl (Netherlands)
- **What:** Smart voice recording device + platform for industrial environments
- **Technology:** Push-to-talk device; workers speak material consumption, production numbers, changeover times; data processed and available by break time. Advanced noise-cancellation for factory floors. Military-grade encryption; recordings auto-anonymized within 24 hours
- **Stage:** Early-stage startup
- **Differentiation:** Dedicated hardware device for industrial voice capture; strong privacy/security focus

### 4.3 Annovox AI
- **What:** Voice-driven video SOP creation for manufacturing
- **Technology:** Workers record video on shop floor; Annovox team + AI turns raw footage into structured video training, SOPs, and multilingual learning. Hands-free voice commands for edits. First drafts delivered in 24-48 hours
- **Stage:** Early-stage, revenue-generating
- **Differentiation:** Human-in-the-loop approach (trained L&D experts + AI); voice-driven editing; multilingual output

### 4.4 MO Docs AI
- **What:** Voice-to-SOP AI platform
- **Technology:** Converts spoken descriptions of processes into structured Standard Operating Procedures
- **Stage:** Early-stage
- **Differentiation:** Focused purely on the voice-to-document conversion pipeline

## 5. AI-Powered Work Instruction & Knowledge Generation

### 5.1 Dirac (USA)
- **What:** AI platform that auto-generates work instructions from CAD files
- **Product:** BuildOS -- automatically generates animated, interactive work instructions for complex mechanical assemblies from CAD
- **Funding:** $10.7M from Founders Fund and Coatue
- **Partnership:** Strategic partnership with Siemens
- **Customers:** Ancra Aircraft (95% time savings), defense/aerospace companies
- **Key Result:** Reduces work instruction creation from weeks/months to hours/minutes. Captures the remaining 10-20% of undocumented tribal knowledge in structured, reusable formats
- **Stage:** Post-seed, early commercial traction
- **Differentiation:** CAD-to-instruction pipeline; targets high-complexity assembly (aerospace, defense, automotive)

### 5.2 Dovient (USA)
- **What:** AI-powered CMMS with "Knowledge Hub" for tribal knowledge capture
- **Technology:** Technicians add knowledge entries from phones: short video, photo with note, voice recording, or text tip. AI-powered RAG layer sits on top of existing document stores and systems
- **Key Results:** Reduce unplanned downtime 35%, cut repair times 40%
- **Stage:** Growth-stage startup
- **Differentiation:** Embedded knowledge capture in daily maintenance workflows (vs. separate documentation effort); mobile-first

### 5.3 Harmony AI (USA)
- **What:** AI automation platform for manufacturing that turns tribal knowledge into digital systems
- **Technology:** Captures operator voice, video, and notes in the moment; indexes them; makes plant tribal knowledge searchable. Connects to ERP, MES, QMS, machines, PLCs, sensors. On-site engineers visit factories to identify "where knowledge is leaking"
- **Business Model:** ~70% from base platform; ~30% customized to each plant
- **Stage:** Growth-stage startup
- **Differentiation:** Hybrid approach with on-site engineers + AI; "white glove" factory-by-factory deployment

### 5.4 WorkCell (Detroit, Michigan, USA)
- **What:** Manufacturing operating system for job shops and contract manufacturers
- **Technology:** AI accumulates knowledge from operators through natural interactions. Analyzes production data to surface undocumented process variations (e.g., day shift vs. night shift parameter differences)
- **Stage:** Early/growth-stage startup
- **Differentiation:** Passive knowledge capture from daily operations data; designed for job shops

### 5.5 UptimeAI (USA)
- **What:** AI Reasoning Agents for industrial operations and manufacturing
- **Technology:** Connects thousands of machines, systems, and processes; learns from operators' daily interactions to build knowledge base. Tracks what operators look at, what maintenance actions they take, and how quickly issues are resolved
- **Key Feature:** "Democratizes tribal knowledge" by labeling failures and learning from thousands of operators' actions
- **Stage:** Growth-stage
- **Differentiation:** Focus on predictive maintenance + tribal knowledge combination; targets the 70% of maintenance personnel over 50 years old

### 6.6 Honeywell Forge

## 10. RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation) in Manufacturing

### Production Deployments
- **Industrial-scale RAG for automotive:** 98.2% extraction accuracy with complete traceability, outperforming baselines by 24.4% and 19.6% (arxiv 2603.20534)
- **Manufacturing safety chatbot:** RAG grounded in OSHA, NIOSH, and OEM manuals for multimodal safety guidance (arxiv 2511.11847)
- **Ceramic tile manufacturing:** RAG-based quality control system that diagnoses defects and proposes solutions
- **Multiple platforms** (Dovient, Augmentir, eGain) now use RAG as baseline architecture for knowledge retrieval

### Challenges
- Standard RAG insufficient for extracting deep, domain-specific knowledge and performing logical reasoning
- PIKE-RAG approach: specialized knowledge extraction + rationale construction for industrial domains
- SME-specific considerations: agile RAG implementation methods needed for smaller manufacturers (arxiv 2508.21024)

## 13. Key Gaps and Observations

### What EXISTS and is DEPLOYED:
1. Connected worker platforms are MATURE and widely deployed (Poka: 2,300+ customers; MaintainX: 11,000+)
2. AR-based expert capture (PTC Vuforia, MS Guides) is in production at scale
3. Digital work instructions and SOPs are well-established
4. RAG for manufacturing documents is operational
5. Big tech industrial copilots (Siemens, SAP, Microsoft) are in expanding pilot/early production

### What is EMERGING (funded but early):
1. Tacit knowledge-specific startups (Interloom, Blockbrain, Murray Mentor) -- all funded 2024-2026
2. Voice-first shop floor capture (Datch, Taggl, Annovox) -- Series A or earlier
3. AI-driven context graphs that map how decisions actually get made (Interloom)
4. "Digital apprenticeship" models with real-time in-ear guidance (Murray Mentor)
5. CAD-to-instruction generation (Dirac) -- strategic but niche

### What is STILL MISSING:
1. **Episodic memory for manufacturing agents** -- no commercial solution captures and reasons over temporal sequences of events (the "why" behind a sequence of decisions over time)
2. **Cross-organizational knowledge transfer** -- all solutions are single-organization; no platform facilitates industry-wide knowledge sharing while protecting IP
3. **Temporal versioning of expertise** -- knowledge changes as processes evolve; no solution tracks how expert knowledge itself evolves over time
4. **Deep causal reasoning** -- systems capture "what" experts do but rarely model "why" (causal mental models)
5. **Collaborative memory across shifts/teams** -- shift handoffs are addressed but collective memory spanning months/years is not
6. **Governance and security** -- mentioned by few (Taggl, Blockbrain) but not deeply solved

### Critical Observation for SE Paper:
The manufacturing knowledge capture space in 2026 mirrors the software engineering memory gap identified in the agentic-memory-se paper:
- **Procedural knowledge is addressed** (work instructions, SOPs -- analogous to code documentation)
- **Episodic memory is absent** (no system captures the temporal, experiential trajectory of problem-solving)
- **Temporal mechanisms are nearly nonexistent** (no versioning of how expertise evolves)
- Connected worker platforms capture snapshots but not the dynamic, evolving nature of tacit knowledge
- This directly parallels the 59% episodic / 4% temporal paradox found in the ICLR MemAgents workshop analysis
